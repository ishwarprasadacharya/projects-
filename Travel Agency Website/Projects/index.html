bastar district

Tourist Places

Bastar district is full of dense forests, heights, hills, waterfalls, caves and wild animals. Bastar Mahal, Bastar Dussehra, Dalpat Sagar, Chitrakot Waterfall, Tirathgarh Waterfall, Kutumsar and Kailash Cave etc. are the main centers of tourism. Historically the region is Dandakaranya in the epic Ramayana and part of the Kosala kingdom in the Mahabharata.

Bijapur

Tourist Places

Its four block divisions are Bijapur, Bhairamgarh, Bhopalpatnam and Usoor. Most of the district consists of hills. The highest peak in the district is Bailadila, also known as “Bull's Hump”. This district is situated to the south of the Indravati River and flows north-south. Bijapur district is rich in forests. The forests found in the district fall under the dry zone which includes mixed forest area.

Bastar Dussehra

Bastar Dussehra is a 75-day festival that takes place in the Bastar district of Chhattisgarh, India. It's a celebration of tribal culture that's different from other Dussehra festivals in India and is sometimes called the world's longest festival. The festival is believed to have begun in the 13th century during the reign of King Purshottam Dev, the 4th King of Bastar. .
